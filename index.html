This is me and what I'm all about

I’m a full-time junior at CUNY City Tech, eager to begin my journey as a software developer. To explore what a career in tech truly involves, I’ve been fortunate to join TTPR. Although my experience with web development is limited, I’m driven by curiosity and a strong desire to learn.

Although I have limited experience as a software developer, I’ve already completed three projects that have helped me grow significantly. One of them is this personal website, which has taught me the importance of layout structure—specifically how to give elements their own space and adjust positioning without compromising design aesthetics. The second project was a basic financial tracker. Its main purpose was to take a user's income—regardless of pay frequency—and convert it into weekly and yearly values. It also allowed users to categorize and total their expenses as either negotiable or non-negotiable, ultimately helping them visualize potential savings by rearranging their spending priorities. Currently, I’m working on a sports-focused platform designed to help users connect with others, discover tournaments, find coaches, and build friendships—all aimed at fostering growth through shared athletic interests.

I love to travel to immerse myself in the cuisine, culture, and rhythm of life in other countries. It gives me a sense of peace amid the nonstop demands of work, school, internships, and personal responsibilities. Meeting new people, even briefly, often feels like a spontaneous adventure—especially when I get invited into someone else's plans or traditions. What excites me most, though, is the dramatic change in scenery. Unlike the urban pace of New York City, many places offer waterfalls, hills, flower fields, serene lakes, open countryside, and ancient ruins—landscapes that refresh both mind and spirit. Learning the history of these places is equally rewarding, especially when they’re ones I first discovered through video games, movies, documentaries, or school. There’s always more depth, more humanity, and more wonder than I expected
